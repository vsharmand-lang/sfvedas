            <p>1. A business case for Salesforce shows an annual licence cost of £500,000 for 200 users. The implementation is described as a "one-time cost" of £300,000. What is most likely wrong with this picture?</p>
            <div class="quiz-options">
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q1','wrong')">A. The user count is too low — 200 users should cost more than £500,000</div>
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q1','right')">B. The implementation cost of £300,000 is likely a significant underestimate (should be closer to £1.5M-2.5M for a typical enterprise programme at this scale), and the ongoing run cost after go-live is missing entirely</div>
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q1','wrong')">C. The licence cost is too high — 200 users at Enterprise pricing should be cheaper than £500,000</div>
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q1','wrong')">D. The implementation should be phased to avoid the one-time cost in Year 1</div>
            </div>
          </div>

          <div class="quiz-question" id="q2">
            <p>2. An organisation in a regulated industry is evaluating Salesforce. Their security team identifies a requirement for field-level encryption for sensitive customer data. What cost implication should the business case include?</p>
            <div class="quiz-options">
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q2','wrong')">A. No additional cost — field-level encryption is included in all Salesforce licences</div>
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q2','wrong')">B. A one-time configuration cost to enable encryption in the org settings</div>
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q2','right')">C. Salesforce Shield licence costs — Shield Platform Encryption is a separately priced add-on that can add significantly to the base licence cost</div>
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q2','wrong')">D. Additional infrastructure costs to run an encrypted database instance</div>
            </div>
          </div>

          <div class="quiz-question" id="q3">
            <p>3. Why should a TCO model cover at least 5 years rather than just Year 1?</p>
            <div class="quiz-options">
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q3','wrong')">A. Because Salesforce requires a 5-year minimum contract commitment</div>
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q3','right')">B. Because ROI typically only becomes measurable in Year 2-3, the cost profile changes substantially year-on-year, and a Year-1-only view dominated by implementation costs presents a misleading picture of long-term economics</div>
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q3','wrong')">C. Because 5-year models are standard accounting practice for all IT investments</div>
              <div class="quiz-option" onclick="answer(this,'q3','wrong')">D. Because Salesforce licence prices are locked for 5 years at initial contract signing</div>
