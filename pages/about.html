The Philosophy

Ancient wisdom.
Modern Salesforce.

The ancient Vedas were not instruction manuals. They were not step-by-step guides for completing a task. They were repositories of deep wisdom — frameworks for understanding the world at its most fundamental level.

Most Salesforce tutorials are instruction manuals. Click here. Configure this. Deploy that. They teach you what to do, but not why the platform was designed this way, what the tradeoffs are, and when to challenge the assumptions entirely.

SFVedas exists for the leaders who need the deeper layer. The CTOs who need to decide whether to bet their enterprise on Salesforce. The delivery managers who need to lead complex implementations without being ambushed by architectural surprises. The solution architects who need to understand the platform's constraints before designing systems that will live for a decade.

"Just as the ancient Vedas were the deepest repository of knowledge in their time — SFVedas is the deepest repository of Salesforce knowledge for today's tech leaders."

This is not a site about getting Salesforce certified quickly. This is a site about understanding Salesforce deeply — the architecture decisions, the delivery realities, the strategic choices that determine whether your investment succeeds or fails.

Vishal Sharma is building SFVedas as he builds his own deep expertise in Salesforce — not as a finished authority, but as a committed practitioner who believes in learning out loud and sharing knowledge at every stage of the journey.

SFVedas is a platform built on a simple belief: that the most valuable knowledge about Salesforce is not found in certification guides or click-through tutorials — it's found in the hard questions that leaders ask in boardrooms, in architecture review meetings, and in the moments before a ₹10 crore implementation decision.

Credentials and background will be built and shared here as the journey continues. What matters today is the quality and depth of the thinking — judge SFVedas by its content, not its resume.
